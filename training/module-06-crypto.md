    <section>
  <h2>1. Cryptocurrency Basics</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Public ledgers mean transactions are often traceable.</li>
    <li>Most mainstream coins are not private by default.</li>
    <li>Exchanges and KYC link blockchain activity to identity.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>2. Privacy Coins</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Coins designed to hide sender, receiver and amount.</li>
    <li>Benefits: harder for third parties to build your financial graph.</li>
    <li>Risks: regulatory attention, exchange delistings, liquidity limits.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>3. Cake Wallet in Practice</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Wallet setup and backup hygiene.</li>
    <li>Compartmenting wallets per identity or campaign.</li>
    <li>Avoiding KYC where possible; if not, understanding the linkage.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>4. Operational Guidelines</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Never mix “clean” and “tainted” funds in the same wallet.</li>
    <li>Consider how on/off-ramps expose you even with privacy coins.</li>
    <li>Document simple workflows instead of improvising under stress.</li>
  </ul>
</section>
