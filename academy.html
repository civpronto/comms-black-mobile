GrapheneOS basics

GrapheneOS is an open‑source, security‑focused mobile OS based on AOSP. It’s hardened against exploits, removes bloat, and gives you granular control. Start by creating a separate owner profile, using a strong screen lock, and installing only what you need via the “Apps” client.

VoIP & anonymous numbers

VoIP lets you call and text over the internet. Combined with privacy‑preserving providers, you can separate identity from everyday communications. Use per‑context numbers (work, travel, classifieds) and disable ID reuse.

Data security

Assume profiling on most networks. Prefer HTTPS, enable DNS‑over‑HTTPS, and keep your browser compartmentalised. Use separate profiles for sensitive tasks, and avoid mixing identities.

Air‑gapped devices

Air‑gapping means keeping a device permanently offline. For ultra‑sensitive workflows, prepare an offline device for note‑taking, crypto signing, or document viewing. Use a Faraday bag when moving it.

Faraday protection

Faraday bags block radio signals (cellular, Wi‑Fi, Bluetooth, NFC). Use them when transporting devices or when you need guaranteed offline behaviour.

Satellite internet

Satellite connectivity (e.g., Starlink) gives you coverage in remote areas or as a censorship fallback. Combine with a privacy router to avoid SIM identity leakage.

Advanced topics

Monero (XMR)

Monero focuses on privacy by default with ring signatures, stealth addresses, and confidential transactions. Treat wallets like keys — back them up and keep seed phrases offline.

OPSEC

Operational security is about protecting your plans and patterns. Separate identities, control information exposure, and keep communication channels compartmentalised.

COMSEC

Communications security keeps messages private and authentic. Prefer end‑to‑end encryption, verify safety numbers, and keep devices updated.

Countersurveillance

Understand likely threats, observe your environment, and reduce metadata. When in doubt, simplify and reduce your attack surface.
