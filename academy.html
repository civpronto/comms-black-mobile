Commsblack Academy

Learn how to secure your communications, protect your privacy, and operate with confidence in a digital-first world.

Core Topics

GrapheneOS Basics

GrapheneOS is an open-source, security-focused mobile OS built on the Android Open Source Project. It’s hardened against exploits, free from unnecessary apps, and puts you in control of what runs on your device. We cover:
- How GrapheneOS differs from standard Android.
- Why verified boot and memory safety matter.
- Getting started with app installation via sandboxed Google Play or open-source alternatives.

VoIP & Anonymous Numbers

Voice over IP lets you make calls and send texts over the internet instead of through a carrier. Providers like Blacktel allow you to operate with anonymous numbers, but costs, quality, and reliability vary. You’ll learn:
- Advantages and limitations of VoIP over SIM-based calls.
- When to use anonymous numbers.
- Managing costs, credits, and recharges.

Data Security

Your browsing and communications are constantly being profiled. Anonymous eSIMs, VPNs, and privacy routers help break the link between you and your activity. Topics include:
- How eSIMs like Silent.link reduce traceability.
- Using a privacy router (IMEI/IMSI/BSSID randomization).
- Threat models: when a VPN helps and when it doesn’t.

Air-Gapped Devices

Air-gapping means keeping a device permanently offline, reducing its attack surface to near-zero. This is critical for handling highly sensitive data. We explore:
- Setting up an air-gapped GrapheneOS device.
- Secure file transfer between offline and online devices.
- Use cases for personal and professional security.

Faraday Protection

Faraday bags block wireless signals, preventing location tracking and unwanted communication. You’ll learn:
- How they work.
- When to store devices inside them.
- Limitations (e.g. accidental signal leakage).

Satellite Internet

Satellite connectivity, like Starlink, allows you to operate outside of cell coverage. With global reach comes new risks and considerations:
- Latency and bandwidth limitations.
- Starlink mini phased-array antennas for mobile use.
- Using satellite safely with privacy tools.

Advanced Topics

Monero (XMR)

Monero (XMR) is a cryptocurrency designed for private, untraceable transactions. Unlike Bitcoin, Monero hides sender, receiver, and transaction amounts. Learn:
- How Monero achieves privacy with ring signatures and stealth addresses.
- Best practices for storing and transacting securely.
- Common mistakes to avoid when using Monero for anonymity.

OPSEC (Operational Security)

OPSEC is about protecting your intentions and activities from being exposed. In practice, it’s about discipline:
- Understanding what information you leak unintentionally.
- Avoiding predictable patterns.
- Building habits that protect identity and intent.

COMSEC (Communications Security)

COMSEC focuses on keeping messages private against interception or tampering. We cover:
- Encryption methods for messaging and calls.
- Secure channels vs. insecure channels.
- Balancing convenience with security.

Countersurveillance

Countersurveillance helps detect and avoid being monitored — digitally or physically. You’ll learn:
- How surveillance works in the real world.
- Techniques for reducing exposure in digital spaces.
- Practical physical-world countersurveillance basics.
