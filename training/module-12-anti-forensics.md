      <h1>Module 12 — Anti-Forensics Awareness</h1>
      <p class="meta">
        Level: Advanced · Recommended for: Shadow / Ghost · Est. time: ~25 minutes
      </p>

      <hr>

    <section>
  <h2>1. What Investigators Look For</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Device images: full copies of storage for later analysis.</li>
    <li>Logs, databases and app caches.</li>
    <li>Network logs, provider records and backups.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>2. Misconceptions</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>"Private mode" or "incognito" does not equal invisibility.</li>
    <li>Deleting an app does not guarantee its data is gone.</li>
    <li>"Eraser" apps can provide more confidence than protection.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>3. Practical Anti‑Forensics</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Use separate devices/profiles for high‑risk roles.</li>
    <li>Limit the number of apps and accounts involved in an operation.</li>
    <li>Use the duress PIN as a planned, rehearsed response to live seizure.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>4. Limits &amp; Ethics</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>No anti‑forensics is perfect, especially against state‑level actors.</li>
    <li>Consider legal and ethical boundaries in your jurisdiction.</li>
    <li>The goal is risk reduction, not invincibility.</li>
  </ul>
</section>
