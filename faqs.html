Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Commsblack Mobile kits, services, and support.

Do I need a subscription to use Commsblack Mobile?

No. Commsblack Mobile does not charge a subscription. However, some features in the communication kits rely on third-party providers such as Silent.link (data eSIM), Blacktel (VoIP services), or Starlink (satellite internet). Any ongoing costs come directly from those providers, not from Commsblack Mobile.

What’s included with each communication kit?

Every kit comes with a GrapheneOS phone, protective accessories, charging equipment, and (depending on the package) privacy hardware and anonymous connectivity.

  • Anonymous Aussie, Operator Aussie, and Sovereign Aussie include 10GB of prepaid data on Silent.link and $10 credit for Blacktel VoIP services to get you started.
  • Everyday Aussie does not include prepaid data or VoIP credit, since it is designed for customers using their own SIM.

How do I recharge data or VoIP credit?

You can recharge directly through Silent.link, Blacktel, or Starlink. If you prefer, Commsblack Mobile can guide you through the process or handle the recharge for you if you provide the funds.

How much do ongoing services cost?

  • Silent.link (Data eSIM): About $3 per GB, never expires.
  • Blacktel (VoIP): $90/month for a number, $0.09/min incoming, $0.12/min outgoing, free incoming texts, $0.71 per outgoing text.
  • Starlink (Satellite): $80/month for 50GB or $195/month unlimited.

Can I use my own SIM card instead?

Yes. The Everyday Aussie kit is designed for customers who want to use their own SIM. For other kits, you can still swap in your SIM if preferred, but some privacy features rely on anonymous eSIMs.

Is GrapheneOS difficult to use?

Not at all. GrapheneOS is based on Android, so it feels familiar if you’ve used an Android phone before. The difference is in the security: GrapheneOS removes bloatware and gives you more control over your device.

What kind of support do you provide?

We offer guidance on setup, using your communication kit, and managing third-party services. You’ll always know how to recharge data, VoIP, or satellite services — either by doing it yourself or through our support.
