      <p class="meta">
        Level: Core · Recommended for: Shield / Shadow / Ghost · Est. time: ~20 minutes
      </p>

      <hr>

    <section>
  <h2>1. What Metadata Is</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>"Data about data" — who, when, where, how often.</li>
    <li>Exists even when content is encrypted.</li>
    <li>Often more valuable for profiling than the content itself.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>2. Metadata Awareness in Daily Use</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Message timing and frequency reveal relationships.</li>
    <li>Login IP and device info reveal movement and device graph.</li>
    <li>File names, sizes and access times add context.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>3. Account &amp; Session Hygiene</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Use separate accounts for separate identities.</li>
    <li>Avoid logging into personal accounts from high‑risk devices.</li>
    <li>Restart devices between roles to clear volatile traces.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>4. Traffic Padding &amp; Noise</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Some tools deliberately add noise to timing/volume patterns.</li>
    <li>Even without special tools, avoid unusual spikes that stand out.</li>
    <li>Consistency and simplicity are more important than perfection.</li>
  </ul>
</section>
