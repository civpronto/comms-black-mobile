      <h1>Module 07 — Operational Security (OPSEC)</h1>
      <p class="meta">
        Level: Applied · Recommended for: Shield / Shadow / Ghost · Est. time: ~25 minutes
      </p>

      <hr>

    <section>
  <h2>1. What OPSEC Is</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Protecting information by controlling your behaviour and workflows.</li>
    <li>Assumes your adversary observes patterns over time.</li>
    <li>Good OPSEC is boring, repeatable and documented.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>2. Common Failure Points</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Reusing accounts across roles or devices.</li>
    <li>Logging into personal social accounts from high-risk devices.</li>
    <li>Talking about operations on normal channels "just once".</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>3. Shield Workflow</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Daily-driver with stronger security/privacy.</li>
    <li>Defined set of apps; update and backup routines.</li>
    <li>Clear rules for when not to use Shield (e.g. sensitive whistleblowing).</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>4. Shadow &amp; Ghost Workflows</h2>
  <p>Simple friction vs protection diagram:</p>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Shield:</strong> Low friction, moderate protection.</li>
    <li><strong>Shadow:</strong> Medium friction, higher protection.</li>
    <li><strong>Ghost:</strong> High friction, highest protection.</li>
  </ul>
  <p>
    As risk increases, move "up" this chart, accepting more friction in
    exchange for more protection.
  </p>
</section>
