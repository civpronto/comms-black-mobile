    <section>
  <h2>1. Adversary Types</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Opportunistic criminals and scammers.</li>
    <li>Stalkers and harassers.</li>
    <li>Corporate or investigative adversaries.</li>
    <li>State-level actors.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>2. Capability vs Kit Matching</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Low-capability (scammers, basic criminals) → Shield with good hygiene.</li>
    <li>Medium (targeted harassment, corporate pressure) → Shadow workflows.</li>
    <li>High (state-level, serious investigations) → Ghost plus strict behaviour.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>3. Example Scenarios</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Journalist protecting sources.</li>
    <li>Activist travelling for a campaign.</li>
    <li>Professional handling sensitive corporate data.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>4. Putting It All Together</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Pick your primary adversary type.</li>
    <li>Select the appropriate kit and modules to focus on.</li>
    <li>Document your personal playbook and keep it simple.</li>
  </ul>
</section>
